About Me

Hi there! I'm Jason Chiu, and I'm a student at the University of California, Irvine.

I major in Computer Game Science and am looking to minor in Business Management.
I develop simple but challenging games, manage and mentor individuals who wish to enter the world of game development, and learn everything I can lay my hands on, whether it be 3D animation with Iclone/3DxChange, front-end web development with HTML/CSS, or marketing and project management - and I look to perfect and master everything I do.
I was one of the organizers the first HackUCI; which drew over 300+ attendees. HackUCI was the first student-organized hackathon in Orange County, and lasted for 36 hours. Attendees worked on a variety of projects ranging from web applications to puzzle games.

I'm currently employed as a Campus Ambassador for Readyforce for my school, and I represent Readyforce at job / career fairs. Readyforce is very similar to LinkedIn, and better tailored to students in Engineering and Computer Science; I focus on increasing the number of students using this service!
I'm also a research assistant at the California Plug Load Research center, where I make animations and develop an interactive application for the Children's Hospital, Orange County (CHOC). This application is developed using Unity2D, and I use the iClone3D and 3dXchange pipline to create animations, while integrating content with the rest of the application.

I've also quite involved in campus activites; I'm the Vice President of the Video Game Design Club @ UCI, which is an organization that helps aspiring game developers network with potential employers and opportunities, and make games.
I'm also the Webmaster of the Taiwanese American Organization @ UCI, which is a club that promotes Taiwanese Culture and helps connect fellow Taiwanese Americans with each other.
In my spare time, I enjoy League of Legends, Hearthstone, piano improvisation, and iced coffee.

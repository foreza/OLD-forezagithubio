Playing with your food can be a valuable learning experience.

In "FoodRun", two players will direct their respective characters in a battle to show each other who is the new cool kid in town.
Both players will have tomatoes and (soon!) other articles of food as their disposal to lob at each other to reduce their health totals.

The game encourages smart usage of your artillery - every time you throw something, you consume a bit of energy!
One player holds a controller, and another person uses the keyboard.

FoodRun was developed in Python, utilizing the Pygame library.
My Role, and future development:

I was the producer on the team. Originally, this project came into being when several students new to the Video Game Development club were unable to find projects. I stepped up and lead several of these students in a quarter long project to make a game just for them and equip them with weapons they could use for making games in the future.
I introduced food as an icebreaker at our first meeting, and this ended up becoming our focus for the development of this game. I kept the team well equipped with frequent discussions and the tools they needed to succeed, such as introducing a repository and having weekly jam sessions as a team.
Development is currently in hiatus as most of the members have decided to work on a different project in Unity, at my suggestion.

Controls:

Movement: Player 1: Arrow Keys Player 2: X-Box Controller
